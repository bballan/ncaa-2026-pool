🏥 Injuries Since the Tournament Started

Two key injuries during tournament play could reshape the bracket:

Iowa St. — Joshua Jefferson (ankle) CRITICAL
The All-American sprained his left ankle in the opening minutes of Friday's first-round win over Tennessee State. He's considered unlikely to play today vs. Kentucky. X-rays were negative, but he couldn't put weight on it leaving the court. Iowa St. is the #2 seed in the Midwest — 108 brackets have them advancing, and 3 have them winning the championship (including #9 Jason Feirman). If Jefferson can't go, Kentucky suddenly has a real shot at a massive upset.
Duke — Caleb Foster (foot), Patrick Ngongba (foot) WATCH
Foster fractured his foot during the first-round win over TCU — he had surgery and is out until at least the Final Four, described as a "minuscule" chance of return. Ngongba played 13 minutes vs. TCU and scored 4 points but was seen in a boot afterward. Duke has 17 championship picks and 129 brackets with them advancing — their depth will be tested in the Sweet 16 against Michigan St.

Sunday's Second Round — What to Watch For

Eight more games today complete the Round of 32 and set the Sweet 16 field. Here's what matters most for the pool:

POOL SWINGER St. John's vs Kansas — 5:15 PM CBS

St. John's (79 picks) vs Kansas (51 picks)
Pool impact: The top 10 is split dead even 5-5. Current leader Nisha Malani has St. John's; #2 mike laning (both brackets) has Kansas. And #6 Ariel Szulc has St. John's winning the entire tournament. A St. John's win supercharges the leader and puts a contrarian championship pick in play. A Kansas loss kills 2 championship brackets.

The Red Storm arguably should have been seeded higher. This game could determine whether Ariel Szulc's moonshot St. John's championship pick stays alive — the highest-ceiling play in the top 10.

POOL SWINGER Virginia vs Tennessee — 6:10 PM TNT

Virginia (73 picks) vs Tennessee (50 picks)
Pool impact: Top 10 split is 3-7 in favor of Tennessee. Seven of the top 10 — including Nisha Malani, both mike lanings, and Ariel Szulc — have Tennessee. Only Michael Stein, Michael Shapiro, and Peter Bernstein have Virginia. A Virginia win would be a massive boost for those three while the rest of the top 10 takes a hit.
The storyline: Virginia's Tony Bennett retired and handed the reins to Ron Sanchez, who has the Cavaliers in the tournament. Tennessee beat Miami (Ohio) 78-56 in Round 1 behind a deep roster. This is a style clash: Virginia's trademark slow pace against Tennessee's athleticism and depth.

INJURY WATCH Iowa St. vs Kentucky — Midwest

Iowa St. (108 picks) vs Kentucky (18 picks)
