Saturday's Second Round — What to Watch For

Eight games today will cut the field from 32 to 24. Here's what matters most for the pool:

POOL SWINGER Michigan St. vs Louisville — 2:45 PM CBS

Michigan St. (90 picks) vs Louisville (28 picks)
Line: Michigan St. favored
Pool impact: 7 of the top 10 brackets have Michigan St. advancing. Only 3 (Jason Feirman, Larry Manis, Jarod Were) picked Louisville. A Louisville win would be a major swing for those three contrarians.
Future stakes: 4,360 pool points riding on Michigan St. across deeper rounds. Justin Bias (#13) has them winning the whole tournament.
The storyline: Michigan State's Jeremy Fears Jr. leads the nation in assists at 9.2 per game, and his 11-assist performance against North Dakota State tied for second-most in MSU tournament history. Louisville's Ryan Conwell is a pure scorer that Fears himself respects from their Big Ten/Big East days. The Spartans' dominant offensive rebounding could exploit Louisville's defensive lapses (South Florida grabbed 18 offensive boards against the Cardinals).

POOL SWINGER Duke vs TCU — 5:15 PM CBS

Duke (129 picks) vs TCU (3 picks)
Line: Duke -10.5
Pool impact: Every single top-10 bracket has Duke. A TCU upset would be catastrophic for the pool — 22,600 future pool points wiped out. Only 3 people in the entire pool picked TCU to advance here.
The storyline: Duke is wounded. Starting PG Caleb Foster and center Patrick Ngongba are both sidelined with foot injuries, and Ngongba's status for Saturday is murky at best. TCU shot 39% from deep against Ohio State, and if they shoot like that again, they could exploit Duke's depleted backcourt. Cam Boozer will need to carry a heavier load. This is the upset watch of the day.

POOL SWINGER Gonzaga vs Texas — 7:10 PM TBS

Gonzaga (85 picks) vs Texas (16 picks)
Line: Gonzaga -5.5
Pool impact: 7 of the top 10 have Gonzaga; 2 have Texas (both mike laning brackets at #3). A Texas upset would give mike laning a huge edge over the rest of the top 10.
Future stakes: 5,600 pool points riding on Gonzaga across deeper rounds. 32 brackets already have dead picks here (they picked BYU or Kennesaw St.).
The storyline: Texas has been the tournament's best story so far — they needed a last-second First Four win over NC State, then dismantled BYU. Their 7-footer Matas Vokietaitis has elevated his game, while Dailyn Swain and Tramon Mark match up well athletically with Gonzaga's guards. But Gonzaga's top-10 defense (per KenPom) is a massive step up from anything Texas has faced.

CLOSEST GAME Nebraska vs Vanderbilt — 8:45 PM TNT

Vanderbilt (84 picks) vs Nebraska (42 picks)
Line: Vanderbilt -2.5 (tightest spread of the day)
Pool impact: The top 10 is split dead even 5-5. Current leader steve odabashian has Vanderbilt; #2 Nisha Malani has Nebraska. This is the game most likely to decide who among the current leaders gains or loses ground.
Future stakes: Nearly equal — 680 points on Vanderbilt, 720 on Nebraska. A true coin flip for the pool.
The storyline: Nebraska earned its first-ever NCAA Tournament win in program history on Thursday, with Pryce Sandfort setting a program record with 7 threes. This is a schematic chess match between Fred Hoiberg (Nebraska) and Mark Byington (Vanderbilt). The Cornhuskers were scorching from three (14-of-39), and if they shoot like that again, the Commodores are in trouble.

Pool impact: All 10 of the top-10 brackets have Houston. 18,120 future pool points on Houston. An A&M upset would be one of the most impactful results of the entire tournament for this pool. But experts aren't giving A&M much of a shot.

Illinois vs VCU — 7:50 PM CBS

Illinois (90 picks) vs VCU (13 picks)
Line: Illinois -10.5
Pool impact: 6 of the top 10 have Illinois. Four — including both mike laning brackets (#3), Michael Shapiro (#7), and Jarod Were (#9) — have the VCU Cinderella continuing. A VCU win here would be a serious boost for those four.
The storyline: VCU completed the largest first-round comeback in NCAA tournament history, overcoming a 19-point second-half deficit to stun North Carolina in overtime. Can the Rams ride that magic against an Illinois team that blew out Penn by 35, led by David Mirkovic's 29 points and 17 rebounds?

Arkansas vs High Point — 9:45 PM TBS

Arkansas (81 picks) vs High Point (4 picks)
Line: Arkansas -11.5 (biggest spread of the day)
Pool impact: 6 of the top 10 have Arkansas. Current leader steve odabashian has a dead pick here (he picked Wisconsin), so this game can't help him. 48 brackets total already have dead picks in this slot. Only 4 people in the entire pool have High Point's Cinderella run continuing.
The storyline: High Point's first-ever March Madness win came via 21 points in the final 5 minutes against Wisconsin, including a go-ahead layup from Chase Johnston (who averaged just 6.1 PPG). They enter with the nation's longest winning streak at 14 games. But Arkansas rolled Hawaii 97-78 behind freshman Darius Acuff Jr.'s 24 points and 7 assists — setting the school's single-season assist record with 223.
